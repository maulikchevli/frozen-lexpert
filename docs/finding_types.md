# Finding-type taxonomy

Every label across the paper's three cohorts is mapped to one of six radiological **phenotypes** by imaging appearance, plus an *uncategorised* bucket for labels that fit no class. This taxonomy is the unit of analysis for the finding-type figures and the Kendall-W difficulty-concordance statistic. For the two public cohorts (RAD-ChestCT, CT-RATE) it is generated by `scripts/paper_build_concept_features.py` and shipped as the machine-readable `results/paper/concept_features.csv`; this file is a human-readable view of the same mapping.

The **Internal cohort (CT)** rows list that cohort's label names for completeness of the taxonomy only. The internal cohort itself — its scans, per-label prevalences, predictions, and results — is a restricted dataset and is **not** part of this release; its prevalence column is intentionally left blank.

Total: **197 labels** — 110 public (92 RAD-ChestCT + 18 CT-RATE) + 87 Internal cohort (CT).

| Phenotype (`finding_type`) | Definition | Public | Internal | Total |
|---|---|--:|--:|--:|

| **Devices** (`device_foreign`) | support devices & foreign material (e.g. pacemakers, stents, tubes) | 19 | 11 | 30 |
